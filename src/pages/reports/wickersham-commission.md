*Official title: Report on the Enforcement of the Prohibition Laws of the United States. Issued by the National Commission on Law Observance and Enforcement, dated January 7, 1931.*

The Wickersham Commission — formally the National Commission on Law Observance and Enforcement, chaired by former U.S. Attorney General George W. Wickersham — was appointed by President Herbert Hoover to investigate the enforcement of Prohibition under the Eighteenth Amendment. The commission's January 1931 report found serious failures in enforcement, widespread corruption, and public non-compliance, while stopping short of recommending outright repeal. Its individual commissioners' dissenting and concurring statements, several of which called for modification or repeal, became as influential as the report itself.
