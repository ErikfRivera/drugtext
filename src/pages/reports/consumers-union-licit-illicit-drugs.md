*By Edward M. Brecher and the Editors of Consumer Reports Magazine, 1972.*

*Licit and Illicit Drugs* is one of the most comprehensive and influential drug-policy reports ever produced. Commissioned by Consumers Union — publishers of *Consumer Reports* — and written by Edward M. Brecher, it examines the full spectrum of psychoactive substances used in America, from opiates and alcohol to LSD and marijuana, applying the same evidence-based consumer-protection framework the organization used for household products. The report's 70 chapters span historical origins, pharmacology, social context, law, and policy recommendations, and remain essential reading in drug-policy research more than fifty years after publication.
