This is a historical comparative-risk graph from the drug library. It plots the **risk of death — measured as deaths per year per 100,000 people at risk** — for a range of common activities and substances, so the relative mortality of drug use can be read against everyday baseline risks. The original page in the [Schaffer Library of Drug Policy](/library/heroin) was a single scanned chart with no accompanying text; the graphic itself is the content, reproduced below.
