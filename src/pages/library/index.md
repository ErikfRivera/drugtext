The Drugtext Library is a historic collection of drug-policy, harm-reduction, and pharmacology documents drawn from the archives of the Institute for the Study of Drug Dependence (ISDD) and the National Institute on Alcohol and Drug Abuse (NIAD). The texts span government commission reports, academic monographs, clinical studies, and investigative works published between the 1960s and early 2000s. They are presented here in full, as primary historical sources.

## Flagship Works

Landmark documents in the collection:

- [*The Politics of Heroin in Southeast Asia* — Alfred W. McCoy (1972)](/library/heroin/politics-of-heroin-southeast-asia) — the landmark investigation into CIA involvement in the Southeast Asian heroin trade
- [*Marihuana: The First 12,000 Years* — Ernest L. Abel](/library/cannabis/marihuana-the-first-12000-years) — a comprehensive history of cannabis from prehistory to the modern era
- [MDMA: A Substance Abuse Textbook Chapter](/library/mdma/substance-abuse-textbook) — a detailed clinical and pharmacological profile of MDMA from a substance abuse textbook
