> "All laws which can be violated without doing anyone any injury are laughed at. Nay, so far are they from doing anything to control the desires and passion of man that, on the contrary, they direct and incite men's thoughts toward those very objects; for we always strive toward what is forbidden and desire the things we are not allowed to have. And men of leisure are never deficient in the ingenuity needed to enable them to outwit laws framed to regulate things which cannot be entirely forbidden.... He who tries to determine everything by law will foment crime rather than lessen it."
>
> — **Spinoza**

This section of the Drugtext library collects primary legal and policy documents relating to drug control: UN treaties, European Union materials, national legislation, international jurisprudence and commentaries, United Nations agency reports, and documents on the intersection of HIV/AIDS policy and drug law.

## Section Contents

- **Treaties on Drugs & Human Rights** — International drug-control conventions and human rights instruments, including the UN Single Conventions and their commentaries. See the already-rebuilt [Commentary on the Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs, 1961](/reports/commentary-single-convention-on-narcotic-drugs-1961) — the UN Secretary-General's article-by-article analysis of the foundational international drug-control treaty.

- **European Union Documents** — EU directives, resolutions, and policy documents on drug control and harm reduction.

- **International Reports** — Major government and inter-governmental commission reports on drug policy. See the [Library](/library) for individual reports.

- **Legislation & Legal Proposals** — National drug laws and proposed legislative reforms from multiple jurisdictions.

- **Jurisprudence, Precedent & Commentaries** — Court decisions, legal commentaries, and scholarly analysis of drug law.

- **INCB, UNDCP & WHO** — Documents from the International Narcotics Control Board, the UN Office on Drugs and Crime, and the World Health Organization.

- **AIDS/HIV & Law** — The intersection of drug law, needle-exchange policy, and the HIV/AIDS epidemic.

## About This Collection

This legal library was assembled by the **Stichting Foundation for Drug Policy and Human Rights** (Drugtext Foundation), Amsterdam, The Netherlands. The collection was established in 1995 with the mission of making primary drug-law documents freely accessible to researchers, policymakers, legal practitioners, and the public.
