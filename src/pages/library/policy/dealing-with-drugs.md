*Edited by Ronald Hamowy. Published by the Pacific Research Institute for Public Policy, San Francisco, 1987.*

*Dealing with Drugs: Consequences of Government Control* brings together nine essays examining the history, economics, medicine, and ethics of United States drug policy. Writing from libertarian and reform perspectives, the contributors argue that prohibitionist drug laws produce consequences more harmful than the drugs themselves, and that market-oriented and harm-reduction approaches deserve serious consideration.
