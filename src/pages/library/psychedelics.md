The Psychedelic Library is Drugtext's collection of historic academic and clinical writings on psychedelic substances — LSD, psilocybin, mescaline, ayahuasca, ibogaine, and related compounds. The collection spans several decades of research and spans therapeutic applications, pharmacology, anthropology, law, and personal accounts. Articles range from 1950s clinical studies to contemporaneous legal analyses of ayahuasca and entheogenic rights. The library also holds two book-length works in full: *The Psychedelics* (edited by Aaronson and Osmond) and *The Varieties of Psychedelic Experience*.

See also: [2C-B](/library/psychedelics/2c-b) — [Psychotomimetic Drugs: An Overview](/library/psychedelics/psychotomimetic-drugs-overview)

## Related Collections

These book-length works are also held in the Drugtext archive and will be linked as they are rebuilt:

- ***The Psychedelics*** (edited by Bernard Aaronson and Humphry Osmond) — a landmark anthology covering anthropological, religious, therapeutic, and sociological dimensions of psychedelic experience. Chapters include *Drugs and Mysticism*, *The Church of the Awakening*, *Central Nervous Effects of LSD-25*, *LSD and Architectural Design*, and more.

- ***The Varieties of Psychedelic Experience*** — a detailed phenomenological survey of psychedelic states, covering body image, encounters with other persons, non-human worlds, mystical experience, and the role of the guide.

- ***Psychedelic Drugs Reconsidered*** — a comprehensive academic review including chapters on adverse effects, therapeutic uses, legal status, and the future of psychedelic research.
