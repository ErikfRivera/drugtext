*By Athanassios Kafkalides.*

## About This Book

*The Power of the Womb and the Subjective Truth* is a work by the Greek psychiatrist Athanassios Kafkalides exploring the concept of pharmaceutical autopsychognosia — a method of self-knowledge through carefully guided psychedelic drug sessions. The book examines the subjective experience produced by substances such as ketamine hydrochloride, the psychological and therapeutic significance of those experiences, and Kafkalides's broader critique of traditional psychiatry. The title reflects the author's thesis that the womb — as the site of earliest subjective experience — holds a formative power over the adult psyche, and that psychedelic sessions can give access to that primal layer of consciousness.
