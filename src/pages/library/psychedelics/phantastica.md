*Phantastica: Narcotic and Stimulating Drugs* is a landmark 1924 work by the German pharmacologist and toxicologist **Louis Lewin** (1850–1929). First published in German as *Phantastica: Die betäubenden und erregenden Genussmittel* (Berlin, 1924) and translated into English in 1931, it stands as one of the earliest systematic scientific surveys of psychoactive substances from around the world.

Lewin coined the term **"phantastica"** for the class of substances that produce hallucinations and altered perception — a category that predates and anticipates the modern term "psychedelics." The book covers narcotics, euphorics, stimulants, inebrients, and soporifics in a pharmacological and ethnobotanical framework unprecedented for its era. It remains an essential reference in the history of psychopharmacology and drug policy.
